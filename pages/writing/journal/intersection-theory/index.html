Chapter 1: Rational Equivalence

This first chapter introduces some of the most basic concepts of intersection theory, cycles and rational equivalence. The primary result proves that rational equivalence pushes forward under proper morphisms of schemes.

Notation and Conventions

Here Fulton establishes his conventions. Schemes are all algebraic schemes over a field, varieties are integral schemes, subvarieties of schemes are always closed subschemes which are varieties and a point on a scheme is always a closed point. He does include a single example in this section, and I'd like to go over it in more detail.

Orders of Zeros and Poles

The setup: XXX is a variety with a subvariety VVV of codimension 1. Thus the local ring A=OV,XA = \mathcal O_{V,X}A=OV,X​ is a one-dimensional local domain. Our goal is to define the order of vanishing of a rational function r∈K(X)×r\in K(X)^\timesr∈K(X)× along VVV. This should be a homomorphism K(X∗)→ZK(X^*) \to \mathbb ZK(X∗)→Z:

ord⁡V(rs)=ord⁡V(r)+ord⁡V(s), \operatorname{ord}_V(rs) = \operatorname{ord}_V(r) + \operatorname{ord}_V(s), ordV​(rs)=ordV​(r)+ordV​(s),

and since r=a/br = a/br=a/b for some a,b∈Aa,b\in Aa,b∈A, we'll necessarily have that

ord⁡V(r)=ord⁡(a)−ord⁡(b). \operatorname{ord}_V(r) = \operatorname{ord}(a) - \operatorname{ord}(b). ordV​(r)=ord(a)−ord(b).

When XXX is nonsingular along VVV then AAA is a DVR, and so any a∈Aa\in Aa∈A can be written utmut^mutm for some unit u∈A×u\in A^\timesu∈A× and a unique m∈Z≥0m\in \mathbb Z_{\geq0}m∈Z≥0​, where ttt is a generator of the maximal ideal. Defining ord⁡V(a)=m\operatorname{ord}_V(a) = mordV​(a)=m then extends to a definition on all of K(X)∗K(X)^*K(X)∗.

Generalizing this definition requires removing both the codimension 1 and the nonsingular hypothesis. In the special case that XXX is a curve over a field KKK and VVV is a point, our "valuation" definition coincides with dim⁡KA/(a)\operatorname{dim}_K A/(a)dimK​A/(a) when a∈Aa\in Aa∈A, but the latter formula works in singular cases too. However, A/(a)A/(a)A/(a) is not a finite KKK-vector space in higher dimensions. According to Fulton, the correct definition is that

ord⁡(a)=lA(A/(a)) \operatorname{ord}(a) = l_A(A/(a)) ord(a)=lA​(A/(a))

where lA(A/(a))l_A(A/(a))lA​(A/(a)) is the length of A/(a)A/(a)A/(a) as an AAA-module. Fulton also write eA(a,A)e_A(a,A)eA​(a,A) for lA(A/aA)l_A(A/aA)lA​(A/aA).

If ord⁡V(r)\operatorname{ord}_V(r)ordV​(r) is positive, then rrr is zero along VVV. If ord⁡V(r)\operatorname{ord}_V(r)ordV​(r) is negative, then rrr "has VVV as a pole", i.e. the denominator of rrr vanishes along VVV.

0⊆mn/(r)⊊mn−1/(r)⊊...⊊m1/(r)⊊A/(r)\begin{aligned} 0 \subseteq \mathfrak m^{n}/(r)\subsetneq \mathfrak m^{n-1}/(r) \subsetneq ... \subsetneq \mathfrak m^{1}/(r) \subsetneq A/(r) \end{aligned}0⊆mn/(r)⊊mn−1/(r)⊊...⊊m1/(r)⊊A/(r)​
